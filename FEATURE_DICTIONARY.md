# Feature Dictionary

This document logs all engineered time-series features, providing clear mathematical definitions and the structural logic behind their inclusion in our demand forecasting models.

---

## 1. Time-Series Feature Catalog

| Feature Name | Feature Group | Mathematical Formula / Transformation | Description | Intuition / Domain Rationale |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `lag_t28` | Lags | $Y_{i, t - 28}$ | Sales quantity recorded exactly 28 days prior. | Captures weekly cyclicality exactly one forecast window back. |
| `lag_t56` | Lags | $Y_{i, t - 56}$ | Sales quantity recorded exactly 56 days prior. | Captures demand behavior exactly two forecast windows back. |
| `roll_mean_7` | Rolling Stats | $\frac{1}{7} \sum_{k=0}^{6} Y_{i, t - 28 - k}$ | 7-day rolling mean shifted by 28 days to prevent leakage. | Smooths short-term weekly noise to capture the current demand level. |
| `roll_mean_28`| Rolling Stats | $\frac{1}{28} \sum_{k=0}^{27} Y_{i, t - 28 - k}$ | 28-day rolling mean shifted by 28 days. | Captures intermediate monthly demand trend. |
| `roll_std_28` | Rolling Stats | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{27} \sum_{k=0}^{27} (Y_{i, t - 28 - k} - \mu_{i})^2}$ | 28-day rolling standard deviation shifted by 28 days. | Measures local volatility, crucial for scaling error and stockouts. |
| `dow` | Calendar | $\text{DayOfWeek}(t)$ | Categorical index (0 = Monday, 6 = Sunday) of the target day. | Captures intra-week shopping patterns (e.g., weekend spikes). |
| `month` | Calendar | $\text{Month}(t)$ | Categorical index (1 to 12) of the target day. | Captures seasonal trends (e.g., holiday periods, summer dips). |
| `is_promo` | Marketing | $\mathbb{I}(\text{SalesPrice}_t < \text{BasePrice})$ | Indicator variable if active price is below historical mode. | Captures demand spikes caused by promotional discounts. |
| `sku_revenue` | Financial | $\sum_{t} \text{Quantity}_{i,t} \times \text{UnitPrice}_{i,t}$ | Total revenue generated by SKU $i$ over training history. | Establishes model importance priority matching WRMSSE weights. |

---

## 2. Guidelines for Feature Engineering
1. **Prevent Data Leakage (Crucial)**: 
   - Since we are forecasting a horizon of 28 days, any lag or rolling window features calculated for target date $t$ must only use information available up to date $t - 28$.
   - **Correct**: `df['roll_mean_7'] = df['quantity'].shift(28).rolling(7).mean()`
   - **Incorrect**: `df['roll_mean_7'] = df['quantity'].rolling(7).mean()` (This leaks future actuals during validation/evaluation!).
2. **Handle Extreme Sparsity**:
   - Many SKUs have zero sales on most days. Aggregate rolling metrics over longer periods (e.g., 56, 90, or 180 days) to prevent division by zero or empty values.
3. **Price/Discount Interaction**:
   - Calculate relative discount percentage: `discount_pct = (base_price - active_price) / base_price`. Demand is highly elastic to relative price drops.
