# Competition Rules & Metric Insights

## 1. Core Evaluation Constraints
To ensure valid submissions, all demand forecasts must strictly adhere to the following physical and system rules:

### A. Non-Negative Constraint (`Quantity >= 0`)
- **Rule**: Predictions for future sales volume cannot be negative.
- **Physical Rationale**: Retail stores cannot experience a negative demand for purchase fulfillments in forecasting windows.
- **Action**: All model outputs must be clipped at a lower bound of `0.0`. E.g., `predictions = np.clip(predictions, a_min=0, a_max=None)`.

### B. Handling of Return Transactions
- **Observation**: The historical transaction log (`train.csv`) contains negative sales quantities (`Quantity < 0`). These represent customer return transactions.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - To reconstruct the actual demand timeline, data is grouped by `Date` and `ItemCode`, and the quantities are summed.
  - If returns exceed sales for a specific SKU on a given day, the net aggregated daily quantity will be negative.
  - **Correction**: We apply `.clip(lower=0)` on the daily aggregated values to represent the baseline active consumer demand for that day as `0`, rather than letting negative net values skew historical rolling demand metrics.

## 2. Evaluation Metric: WRMSSE
The competition evaluates submissions using the **Weighted Root Mean Squared Scaled Error (WRMSSE)**, adapted from the M5 Forecasting Competition.

### A. Mathematical Formulation
For a single time series $i$, the Root Mean Squared Scaled Error (RMSSE) is defined as:

$$RMSSE_i = \sqrt{ \frac{ \frac{1}{h} \sum_{t=n+1}^{n+h} (Y_{i,t} - \hat{Y}_{i,t})^2 }{ \frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{t=2}^{n} (Y_{i,t} - Y_{i,t-1})^2 } }$$

Where:
- $Y_{i,t}$ is the actual demand of series $i$ at time $t$.
- $\hat{Y}_{i,t}$ is the forecasted demand of series $i$ at time $t$.
- $n$ is the historical training length.
- $h$ is the forecasting horizon (28 days).

The **WRMSSE** aggregates the individual RMSSE scores across all $N$ series using a weight $w_i$:

$$WRMSSE = \sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i \times RMSSE_i$$

Where:
- $w_i$ represents the weight of series $i$, computed as the proportion of cumulative dollar sales (revenue) generated by SKU $i$ over the entire historical training period relative to total catalog revenue.

---

## 3. Key Intuitions for Modeling
1. **Scale-Independency (RMSSE)**: By dividing the forecast's MSE by the historical one-step-ahead naive forecasting MSE (the denominator), the error is scaled by the inherent volatility of each specific SKU. A forecast error of 10 units on a highly volatile product is penalized less than a 10-unit error on a highly stable product.
2. **Revenue-Driven Optimization (Weights $w_i$)**: The weights $w_i$ are based on financial value (revenue = `Quantity` $\times$ `UnitPrice`). Fast-moving, expensive items have high weights and dominate the final score. Consequently, modeling efforts should prioritize high-value/high-volume SKUs.
3. **Horizon Handling**: The submission requires predicting two separate 28-day windows:
   - `validation`: Days 1-28 post-training.
   - `evaluation`: Days 29-56 post-training.
   A single static mean model is highly effective as a baseline, but sequential models (e.g., recursive forecasting or direct multi-step models) will be required to capture long-term decay and calendar cycles.
